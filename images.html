Cosmic Cliffs

Roughly 7,600 light-years away, at the edge of a nearby, young, star-forming region called NGC 3324 in the Carina Nebula. This image reveals previously invisible areas of star birth by using infrared light.

More on Cosmic Cliffs

Stephan’s Quintet

A visual grouping of five galaxies. This is Webb’s largest image to date. It contains over 150 million pixels and is constructed from almost 1,000 separate image files. Webb captures huge shock waves as one of the galaxies, NGC 7318B, smashes through the cluster. The leftmost galaxy is 40 million light-years away while the other 4 are roughly 290 million light-years away.

More on Stephan’s Quintet

Southern Ring Nebula

The dimmer star at the center of this scene has been sending out rings of gas and dust for thousands of years in all directions. The nebula is approximately 2,500 light-years away.

More on Southern Ring Nebula

Webb’s First Deep Field

This image shows the galaxy cluster SMACS 0723 as it appeared 4.6 billion years ago, with many more galaxies in front of and behind the cluster. Within this deep field image there are thousands of galaxies including the faintest objects ever observed in the infrared.
